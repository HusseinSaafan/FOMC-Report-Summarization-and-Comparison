# FOMC Reports Brief

## January

### Summary

## Snapshot
The Committee maintained the target range for the federal funds rate at 4-1/4 to 4-1/2 percent, emphasizing the need for data-driven assessments regarding future adjustments. Economic activity expanded solidly, with the unemployment rate stabilizing and labor conditions remaining strong. Inflation is noted as elevated, and risks to achieving both employment and inflation goals are balanced. The Committee will continue reducing its holdings of Treasury and agency securities.

## Why It Matters
The statement reflects a cautious yet steady policy stance, underscoring the importance of monitoring incoming data for potential future adjustments. The focus on balanced risks highlights an ongoing commitment to achieving dual mandate objectives.

## March

### Summary

## Snapshot
The Committee maintained the federal funds rate target range at 4 -1/4 to 4 -1/2 percent. Economic activity continues to expand solidly, with stable labor market conditions and elevated inflation. Uncertainty in the economic outlook has increased, prompting a careful assessment of incoming data and risks. The pace of reduction of securities holdings will slow, with a new cap for Treasury securities set at $5 billion monthly beginning in April while agency debt and mortgage-backed securities maintain a $35 billion cap.

## Why It Matters
The FOMC's decision underscores a balanced approach to monetary policy, reflecting an ongoing commitment to maximum employment and inflation control. The adjustment of the Treasury securities redemption cap indicates a strategy to manage the balance sheet alongside economic uncertainties.

### Comparison to Previous Month

## Key Deltas
The current statement introduces an increase in uncertainty regarding the economic outlook, contrasting with the previous assessment of balanced risks. Notably, Christopher J. Waller voted against the action, advocating for no change in the federal funds target range.

## Net Interpretation
The FOMC's language appears more cautious and slightly dovish, reflecting greater uncertainty about economic conditions and a strategic adjustment in balance-sheet reduction.

## May

### Summary

## Snapshot
The Committee decided to maintain the federal funds rate target range at 4-1/4 to 4-1/2 percent, focusing on maximum employment and a 2 percent inflation goal. Economic activity is expanding at a solid pace despite increased uncertainty. Labor market conditions remain solid, while inflation is still somewhat elevated. The risks of higher unemployment and inflation have risen. The Committee will continue to reduce its holdings of Treasury securities and related assets as part of its ongoing operations.

## Why It Matters
The statement emphasizes a cautious approach with no immediate policy adjustment planned, while noting growing risks. Key economic indicators will dictate future policy shifts, making upcoming data critical for assessing the economic outlook.

### Comparison to Previous Month

## Key Deltas
The current statement introduces a specific acknowledgment that the risks of higher unemployment and higher inflation have risen, increasing the caution around the economic outlook. The language on uncertainty has evolved to indicate that it has increased further. The Committee continues reducing its securities holdings without specifying any change in the operational caps since the last statement.

## Net Interpretation
The shifts here read moderately dovish, emphasizing growing risks and caution without altering the current policy rate, suggesting vigilance while maintaining the existing stance.

## July

### Summary

## Snapshot
The Committee maintained the target range for the federal funds rate at 4-1/4 to 4-1/2 percent, reflecting solid labor market conditions and moderate growth of economic activity. Inflation remains somewhat elevated, and uncertainty about the economic outlook is high. The Committee emphasized its commitment to supporting maximum employment and returning inflation to the 2 percent goal. Two members dissented, preferring to lower the rate by 1/4 percentage point.

## Why It Matters
The FOMC's decision reinforces a cautious approach to monetary policy, with a focus on data dependency and a balanced assessment of risks. Continued monitoring of economic indicators is essential for directing future policy adjustments.

## September

### Summary

## Snapshot
The Committee decided to lower the target range for the federal funds rate by 1/4 percentage point to 4 to 4-1/4 percent, citing moderated economic growth and rising downside risks to employment. Inflation remains somewhat elevated, and the Committee continues to focus on achieving maximum employment and a 2 percent inflation target. The balance of risks is elevated, with attention to incoming data influencing future policy adjustments. The Committee is also committed to ongoing reductions in its holdings of Treasury securities and agency debt.

## Why It Matters
The decision reflects a cautious approach amidst economic uncertainty, signaling readiness to adapt policy as new data emerges. Market participants should consider this shift in rate targets as indicative of potential changes in the economic outlook.

### Comparison to Previous Month

## Key Deltas
The current statement indicates a decision to lower the target range for the federal funds rate by 1/4 percentage point to 4 to 4-1/4 percent. The labor market language now reflects slower job gains and a slight uptick in the unemployment rate, coupled with increased emphasis on downside risks to employment. Additionally, the interest rate paid on reserve balances has been reduced to 4.15 percent.

## Net Interpretation
The changes suggest a modestly dovish shift as the Committee responds to rising risks in the labor market, highlighting a more cautious monetary policy stance amid economic uncertainties.

## October

### Summary

## Snapshot
The Committee lowered the target range for the federal funds rate by 1/4 percentage point to 3.75–4 percent, noting that economic activity has expanded moderately. Job gains slowed, the unemployment rate increased slightly, and inflation remains somewhat elevated. The balance of risks is shifting, with rising downside risks to employment highlighted. The Committee will conclude the reduction of its aggregate securities holdings on December 1, 2025, and will continue to assess incoming data closely.

## Why It Matters
This rate adjustment reflects a cautious approach amid ongoing labor market challenges and elevated inflation. The decision signals the Committee's readiness to adapt its policy in response to evolving economic conditions, emphasizing stability in employment and inflation objectives.

### Comparison to Previous Month

## Key Deltas
The Committee lowered the target range for the federal funds rate to 3.75–4 percent, down from 4 to 4.25 percent. The current statement concludes the reduction of aggregate securities holdings by December 1, 2025, while the previous statement did not specify an end date. Additionally, two members dissented in the recent meeting, with one preferring a smaller cut and the other advocating for no change.

## Net Interpretation
The overall tone appears cautiously dovish, reflecting ongoing concerns about the labor market and inflation while indicating a strategic pivot in balance-sheet operations.

## December

### Summary

## Snapshot
The Committee lowered the target range for the federal funds rate by 1/4 percentage point to 3-1/2 to 3-3/4 percent, acknowledging moderate economic expansion but noting elevated inflation levels. Job gains have decelerated, and unemployment has risen slightly. Risks to employment have increased, and the Committee remains committed to maximum employment and the 2 percent inflation goal. The Committee will also initiate purchases of shorter-term Treasury securities to maintain ample reserve levels.

## Why It Matters
This action highlights a cautious approach to monetary policy while adapting to changing economic conditions. The emphasis on incoming data will guide future adjustments, reflecting the Committee's ongoing balancing act between inflation control and employment support.

### Comparison to Previous Month

## Key Deltas
The current statement lowers the federal funds rate target range further to 3-1/2 to 3-3/4 percent, down from 3-3/4 to 4 percent. It explicitly states that the Committee will initiate purchases of shorter-term Treasury securities to ensure ample reserves, a shift from previous plans to conclude reductions of securities holdings by December 1. A dissent is noted with two members preferring no change instead of just one.

## Net Interpretation
The changes reflect a more dovish stance, with a clear shift toward maintaining liquidity in the financial system while continuing to address inflation and employment risks.
